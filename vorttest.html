VORT TEST & LICENSE SYSTEM

In South Australia there are two ways to obtain your Driver's License; the CBT&A (Competency Based Training & Assessment or 'Log Book') System or the VORT (Vehicle On Road Test) System. Learn Pass Drive offers driver training with a licensed Motor Vehicle Driving Instructor under the popular VORT (Vehicle On Road Test) System.


What is the VORT?

The Practical Vehicle on Road Test is a popular way for many South Australian's to obtain their Driver's License. After the completion of driving lessons, students undertake a practical, real-time, on road test with an Accredited Examiner, which is organised by us. During the VORT, the driver must successfully complete the 5 slow speed maneouvres and the General Drive of the test.

The 5 slow speed manoeuvres are: o Moving off on a hill o U-Turn o Reverse Parallel Park o Angle Park o 3 Point Turn · The System of Vehicle Control · Road law compliance · Defensive driving · Safer steering methods · Safe driving strategies such as; rules of observation, rules of braking, vehicle positioning, stopping distance and following distance.

Learn Pass Drive will teach you each of these manoeuvres as well as the required general road law, enabling you to successfully execute them in the final test.

At the completion of each lesson, a Student Record Sheet is updated, capturing all training notes and areas that need finessing. Once driving lessons are completed, we book in your Practical VORT. Our Instructor will progress you through the training at your own pace, as quickly as your learning will allow. Students already familiar with driving may find themselves progressing through all the manouveres relatively quickly.


What is considered a VORT Pass?

To pass the VORT you must successfully demonstrate all 5 slow speed manoeuvres in combination with the General Drive, score a pass mark of 90% or more and not breach any road law during the drive. Breaches of road law will result in an immediate fail.

The VORT system is a very popular way to obtain your license in South Australia. Many students believe it is a quicker method to obtaining their South Australian Driver's License versus the Log Book system.
